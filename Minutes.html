Minutes of Meetings:

Minutes are written records of the proceedings and discussions that occur during meetings. They provide an overview of the topics discussed, decisions made, and actions assigned to participants.

Types of Minutes

  1. Formal Minutes
    1. Board Meeting Minutes
    2. Shareholder Meeting Minutes
  2. Informal Minutes
    1. Team Meeting Minutes
    2. Project Meeting Minutes
  3. Action Minutes
    1. Action-Item Minutes
    2. Follow-Up Meeting Minutes
  4. Verbatim Minutes
    1. Literal Verbatim Minutes
    2. Modified Verbatim Minutes
  5. Summary Minutes
    1. Action-Oriented Summary Minutes
    2. Highlights Summary Minutes

Board Meeting Minutes

Board meeting minutes are a written record of the proceedings, decisions, and actions taken during a board meeting. They serve as an official document to capture the discussions and outcomes of the meeting. Here is an example of how board meeting minutes can be formatted:

Board Meeting Minutes

Date: [Date of the Meeting]

Location: [Meeting Location]

Attendees:

  • [Name 1] - [Position/Title]
  • [Name 2] - [Position/Title]
  • [Name 3] - [Position/Title]

Agenda:

  1. [Agenda Item 1]
  2. [Agenda Item 2]
  3. [Agenda Item 3]

Minutes:

  1. [Agenda Item 1]
    • [Discussion/Summary of Discussion]
    • [Decisions Made]
    • [Actions Assigned]
  2. [Agenda Item 2]
    • [Discussion/Summary of Discussion]
    • [Decisions Made]
    • [Actions Assigned]
  3. [Agenda Item 3]
    • [Discussion/Summary of Discussion]
    • [Decisions Made]
    • [Actions Assigned]

Next Meeting:

The next board meeting is scheduled for [Date of Next Meeting] at [Location of Next Meeting].

Adjournment:

The meeting was adjourned at [Time of Adjournment].

Action-Item Minutes

Action-item minutes are used to document the specific action items assigned during a meeting, along with the responsible parties and deadlines. They serve as a reference for tracking progress and ensuring accountability. Here is an example of how action-item minutes can be formatted:

Action-Item Minutes

Date: [Date of the Meeting]

Location: [Meeting Location]

Attendees:

  • [Participant 1]
  • [Participant 2]
  • [Participant 3]

Action Items:

Action Item Assigned To Deadline
[Action Item 1] [Responsible Party 1] [Deadline 1]
[Action Item 2] [Responsible Party 2] [Deadline 2]
[Action Item 3] [Responsible Party 3] [Deadline 3]

Next Meeting:

The next meeting is scheduled for [Date of Next Meeting] at [Location of Next Meeting].

Adjournment:

The meeting was adjourned at [Time of Adjournment].

Literal Verbatim Minutes

Literal verbatim minutes are a precise record of a meeting, capturing the exact words spoken by participants. They provide a detailed account of the discussions, presentations, and decisions made during the meeting. Here is an example of how literal verbatim minutes can be formatted:

Literal Verbatim Minutes

Date: [Date of the Meeting]

Location: [Meeting Location]

Attendees:

  • [Participant 1]
  • [Participant 2]
  • [Participant 3]

Minutes:

  1. [Start of the Meeting]

    2. [Exact transcript of the conversation]

  2. [Agenda Item 1]

    3. [Exact transcript of the conversation]

  3. [Agenda Item 2]

    4. [Exact transcript of the conversation]

  4. [Agenda Item 3]

    5. [Exact transcript of the conversation]

Next Meeting:

The next meeting is scheduled for [Date of Next Meeting] at [Location of Next Meeting].

Adjournment:

The meeting was adjourned at [Time of Adjournment].
